Real Madrid Football Club History

Foundation and Early Years

Real Madrid grew out of Football Club Sky, a team formed in Madrid in 1897[^1^][3]. The club was officially founded in 1902 and joined the Royal Spanish Football Federation in 1909[^1^][3]. It was originally named Madrid Football Club; it wasn't until 1920 that King Alfonso XIII bestowed them with the title of "real" – or "royal" in English[^2^][1][^3^][2]. These early years were reasonably successful for Real, as evidenced by their four consecutive Copa del Rey victories from 1905 to 1908[^2^][1][^3^][2].

The Bernabéu Era

The post-war period saw Santiago Bernabéu Yeste take over as the club's president[^2^][1]. Faced with a difficult task of rebuilding the club, Bernabéu went on to construct a brand-new stadium – later renamed as the Santiago Bernabéu stadium – and sign a number of high-profile international players in order to compete at the European stage[^2^][1]. With a bonafide dream team centered around the talents of Alfredo Di Stéfano, Ferenc Puskás and Francisco Gento, Real cruised to four La Ligas and one Copa del Rey by the end of the 60s[^2^][1]. More impressively, they claimed the first five European Cups ever organized, handily destroying any opposition in their path from 1956 to 1960[^2^][1].

Recent Success

In addition to their domestic success, Real Madrid's reputation as a major club was established by their outstanding record in the European Cup. To date, they have been crowned champions of Europe a record fourteen times[^2^][1][^3^][2]. They have also won the UEFA Cup twice and were twice runners-up in the European Cup Winners' Cup[^3^][2].

Notable Players

Many of the best players in the world have played in the clubs white shirts, including legends like Alfredo Di Stéfano, Ferenc Puskás, Francisco Gento, Butragueño, Michel, Hugo Sánchez, Fernando Redondo, Luís Figo, Zinedine Zidane, Roberto Carlos, Raúl González, Kaká, Cristiano Ronaldo, Gareth Bale[^2^][1].

Real Madrid Football Club Trophies

International Trophies

Real Madrid has a rich history in international football with a record **32 trophies**[^1^][3]. This includes a record **14 European Cup/UEFA Champions League titles**[^1^][3], a joint record **5 UEFA Super Cups**[^1^][3], **2 UEFA Cups**[^1^][3], a joint record **2 Latin Cups**[^1^][3], a record **1 Iberoamerican Cup**[^1^][3], and a record **8 FIFA Club World championships**[^1^][3].

Domestic Trophies

In domestic football, Real Madrid has won **35 La Liga titles**[^2^][1][^3^][2], **20 Copa del Rey (Spanish Cup)**[^2^][1][^3^][2], **13 Supercopa (Spanish Super Cup)**[^2^][1][^3^][2], and **1 Copa Eva Duarte**[^2^][1][^3^][2].

Other Trophies

Real Madrid has also won other trophies such as **1 League Cup**, **1 Copa Iberoamericana**, **1 International Champions Cup**, **28 Santiago Bernabeu Trophy**, **9 Teresa Herrera Trophy**, **2 Orange Trophy**, **4 Trofeo Ciutat de Palma**, **3 Trofeo Colombino**, **6 Trofeo Carranza LaLiga World**, and **1 Costa del Sol Trophy**[^3^][2].
